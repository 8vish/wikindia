The Empirical Rule, also known as the three-sigma rule or 68-95-99.7 rule, is a statistical principle that provides insights into the distribution of data in a normal distribution. It states that:

  • 68% of data falls within one standard deviation from the mean.
  • 95% of data falls within two standard deviations from the mean.
  • 99.7% of data falls within three standard deviations from the mean.

Our Empirical Rule Calculator simplifies the application of this rule. Input the mean and standard deviation of your dataset, click "Calculate," and interpret the provided intervals, offering both numerical and visual representations.

The significance of the Empirical Rule in statistical analysis lies in its use for probability assessment, data validation, and forecasting. It quickly provides an overview of data distribution, aiding decision-making and predictions.
